When the cost of raw materials increases, what happens to the supply curve and why?

Short Answer
trending_up
check

AI Explanation

Excellent analysis. Higher input prices decrease the profitability of producing the good at any price, causing suppliers to reduce their output. This is graphically represented as a leftward shift of the supply curve (a decrease in supply), not a movement along the curve.
